01 — What I see when I look at Blackbird

The detail is the business model.

You left a Sydney hospitality life to slow down, found an old banana plantation on the side of Mount Koonyum, and reused what was already on the land. Concrete panels, whitewashed ply, Moroccan tiles, rescued railway sleepers, reo mesh, corrugated iron, recycled timber — brutalism and minimalism, the pavilions turned east to Cape Byron and the Pacific.

Even the mark is hand-made: Bruce Goold drew the blackbird in ink and brush, perched on a palm frond to mean home, nest, retreat. Nothing here is off-the-shelf. That is exactly why it works.

The standard isn't decoration on top of the product. It is the product — and the moat. Anyone can buy an infinity edge; almost no one will rebuild from what was already on site and hold the restraint.

  • Network 10 "Hotels by Design", 2020 finalist — praised as "simple, honest, unique" and "in touch with Mother Nature."

04 — The market: where the world is heading

Luxury is moving toward exactly what Blackbird already is.

The big-picture context — and why this property sits ahead of the curve rather than chasing it. The risk isn't that the market moves away from Blackbird; it's that the hinterland fills with people copying it.

Independents are winning

Independent & boutique luxury is growing faster than the branded majors — roughly 8–9% a year. Guests are actively leaving chains for design and story.

~68% choose character

Around two-thirds of travellers now pick unique design and personalised service over a predictable chain room.

~85% reward values

McKinsey finds ~85% of luxury travellers prefer brands aligned with their sustainability values — Blackbird's reuse story is an asset, not a footnote.

Australia: regional surge

National occupancy near 71% and RevPAR around A$171 and climbing — with the sharpest growth in regional, drive-to luxury exactly like the Northern Rivers.

The five forces shaping 2026

  • Quiet luxury — understated, intentional, restorative. Flash is over.
  • Slow travel — fewer, deeper, longer stays; presence over itinerary.
  • Wellness as the reason to travel — no longer an add-on amenity.
  • Regenerative & adaptive reuse — authenticity and sense of place beat any new build.
  • Direct-first — owned story and owned channels replacing OTA dependence.

The hinterland is getting crowded

New design-led players are arriving on Blackbird's doorstep — Sun Ranch, The Brooklet by Cordelia, and The Bonobo by Raes (opening June 2026). The region is becoming genuinely world-class.

Blackbird got here first, in 2018. The job isn't to invent a position — it's to defend and sharpen one the property already holds before the newcomers close the gap.

Market figures: independent boutique/luxury growth and traveller-preference research (Grand View, Fortune Business Insights, McKinsey, 2025); Australian hotel performance (national sector data, 2025). Directional context, not Blackbird figures.
